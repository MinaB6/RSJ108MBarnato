Mina Barnato's Guide to Creating a Photography Portfolio

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make a photography portfolio.

Time Needed: Three to four hours.
Purpose: To be able to show your photography work in an organized manner.

Photography is an important element in Journalism and Graphic Design. Having a portfolio of your photography work or Photoshop projects will help you present your past work and skillset to potential agencies, newspapers, or clients.

  • Things You'll Need

  • A presentation folder or portfolio case of your choice.
  • Printed copies of your work.
  • A free website that you can use as a digital portfolio.
  • Digital copies of your work in the form of jpgs or pngs.

  1. Think about how you will use this portfolio.

    What kind of work are you seeking?

    Who will be interviewing you? What kind of work have you done that will be most relevant to them?

    Which skillsets do you want to highlight? What's your style?

  2. Collect all of your works either digitally or printed that suit how you will use the portfolio.

    Try to keep a general theme or coherence to your selections. Pick your best examples.

    " >

    Photo by Cuba Gallery on Flickr.

  3. Build your online digital portfolio website.

    Think about how the design of the website will complement and organize your body of work. Use a free website builder and prepare it for your photographs. Design the way the images sit on the page or how someone using the site will interact with it.

    This is a tutorial for a free website builder called Weebly. You can view how I used it as a graphic design portfolio right here.

  4. Assemble your printed photographs into your portfolio folder or case.

    Make sure that they are a consistent size and that you used high quality prints so that your work is properly and beautifully highlighted.

    This photographer talks about a photobook company he has worked with before called SAAL Digital.

  5. Send out your portfolio!

    Now that you have both a digital and printed portfolio, you can email your portfolio website to people and then present your printed works when you get an interview.
